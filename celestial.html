Celestial Events

There's more to the night sky than the planets!

Meteor showers happen all the time! As earth orbits around the sun, it travels through debris fields of comets, creating an amazing night time spectacle! Here are some annual meteor showers.

  1. Quadrantids:
    • January 3-4
  2. Lyrids:
    • April 22-23
  3. Eta Aquarids:
    • May 5-6
  4. Perseids:
    • August 11-13
  5. Draconids:
    • October 8-9
  6. Orionids:
    • October 21-22
  7. Leonids:
    • November 17-18
  8. Geminids:
    • December 13-14
  9. Ursids:
    • December 22-23

The moon goes through many phases such as new moon, waxing crescent, first quarter, waxing gibbous, full moon, waning gibbous, last quarter, and waning crescent.

Solar eclipses happen when the moon blocks the sun, and lunar eclipses occur when the earth's shadow covers the moon. These events repeat annually, providing astronomers and skywatchers with predictable celestial phenomena to observe. Depending on where you live around the world depends if you are able to see eclipses. Please check your local news to find celestial events that happen where you live!
