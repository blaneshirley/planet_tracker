The Planets

Our solar system has eight unique and fascinating planets. Most have been known since ancient times, but little were known about their characteristics until recently. Below are some interestics facts about seven of the amazing planets in our solar system that can be viewed from earth!

Mercury:

  • Distance from the Sun: Approximately 57.9 million kilometers (36 million miles)
  • Orbital Period: 88 Earth days
  • Discovery: Known since ancient times
  • Known For: Being the closest planet to the Sun and having extreme temperature variations.
  • Fun Fact: Despite being the closest planet to the Sun, Mercury is not the hottest.

Venus:

  • Distance from the Sun: Approximately 108.2 million kilometers (67.2 million miles)
  • Orbital Period: 225 Earth days
  • Discovery: Known since ancient times
  • Known For: Its extremely thick and toxic atmosphere, making it the hottest planet in the solar system.
  • Fun Fact: Venus rotates in the opposite direction to most planets, a phenomenon known as retrograde rotation.

Mars:

  • Distance from the Sun: Approximately 227.9 million kilometers (141.6 million miles)
  • Orbital Period: 687 Earth days
  • Discovery: Known since ancient times, but telescopic observations provided more details.
  • Known For: Its red appearance due to iron oxide on its surface, and its potential as a future target for human exploration.
  • Fun Fact: Mars has the largest volcano in the solar system, Olympus Mons, and the deepest canyon, Valles Marineris.

Jupiter:

  • Distance from the Sun: Approximately 778.5 million kilometers (484 million miles)
  • Orbital Period: 11.9 Earth years
  • Discovery: Known since ancient times, but detailed observations began with the invention of telescopes.
  • Known For: Being the largest planet in the solar system and having a massive magnetic field.
  • Fun Fact: Jupiter's Great Red Spot is a gigantic storm that has been raging for at least 400 years.

Saturn:

  • Distance from the Sun: Approximately 1.4 billion kilometers (886 million miles)
  • Orbital Period: 29.5 Earth years
  • Discovery: Known since ancient times, but first observed with a telescope by Galileo Galilei in 1610.
  • Known For: Its spectacular ring system, made up of icy particles and debris.
  • Fun Fact: Saturn is the least dense planet in the solar system, meaning it would float if placed in water.

Uranus:

  • Distance from the Sun: Approximately 2.9 billion kilometers (1.8 billion miles)
  • Orbital Period: 84 Earth years
  • Discovery: Discovered by William Herschel on March 13, 1781.
  • Known For: Its unique feature of being tilted on its side, possibly due to a collision early in its history.
  • Fun Fact: Uranus' moons are named after characters from the works of William Shakespeare and Alexander Pope.

Neptune:

  • Distance from the Sun: Approximately 4.5 billion kilometers (2.8 billion miles)
  • Orbital Period: 164.8 Earth years
  • Discovery: Discovered by Johann Gottfried Galle and Heinrich Louis d'Arrest on September 23, 1846.
  • Known For: Being the eighth and farthest known planet from the Sun in the Solar System.
  • Fun Fact: Neptune's atmosphere contains the fastest winds in the solar system, reaching speeds of up to 2,100 kilometers per hour (1,300 miles per hour).
